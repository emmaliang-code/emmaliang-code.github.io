Navigating Managed Retreat in Halifax, NS

Undergraduate Honours Thesis

Dalhousie University, NS

This research examines the concept of managed retreat and stakeholder responsibility in facilitating a coordinated risk reduction strategy against regional sea-level rise. The study investigates three Nova Scotian coastal municipalities that exhibit characteristics requiring managed retreat and the factors facilitating or constraining these strategies. It analyzes the decision-making processes, institutional barriers, and opportunities that influence how communities can develop effective climate adaptation planning.

Research Questions

Primary Research Question

Who is responsible for informing property buyers about climate risks along Halifax's coastline?

Supporting Research Questions

  • How does the perception of property values along Halifax's coastline influence stakeholder attitudes towards managed retreat?
  • What are the key legal and institutional barriers to implementing managed retreat strategies in Halifax?
  • How can communication and collaboration between municipal, provincial, and federal levels of government be improved to facilitate effective climate adaptation planning?
